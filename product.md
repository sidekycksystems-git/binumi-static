## What Binumi is

Binumi is a services-as-software company for enterprise communication. It is **not**
a standalone video software tool, and it is not an agency. It delivers the work
itself, through two components sold as one:

- **The platform (the scale).** A brand-tuned AI video engine that turns documents, briefs, decks and prompts into on-brand video, drawing on 15M+ rights-cleared clips, handling script, footage, voiceover and branding.
- **The service (the expertise and the moat).** A human team that configures the platform around the client's brand, content and approval workflows, brings the storytelling craft, and supplies the judgment that makes each video land. In a domain where quality cannot be machine-verified, this layer is why the outcome can be trusted and charged for.

**The core couplet (use everywhere):**
> The platform is the scale. The service is the expertise.

This is the spine of all messaging. The platform lets a team produce targeted video
at a volume it could never reach alone; the service brings the storytelling craft
that makes each video land.

## Pricing (currently NOT public on the site)

Internal tiers from the sales toolkit:
- **Pilot.** About £5K (the "proof of value" entry motion; about 14 day time-to-value).
- **Business.** About £40K/year (ICP: organizations of about 50 to 250; 3 users plus dedicated account manager).
- **Enterprise.** £100K+/year (CMS/LMS/CRM integration; multinationals; white-label option).

3 year customer maturity model: Year 1 Servicing, Year 2 Team Expansion, Year 3
Impact and Scale.

Whether to publish pricing is an open strategic decision (enterprise norm is to
obscure; the brand argument is to publish at least a starting price to self-qualify
buyers). Not a content-calendar decision, but useful context.

**Pricing direction (services-as-software model):** lead toward per-deliverable and per-program pricing, the agency model at software margins, rather than literal pay-per-result. The outcome (a video that lands) is not cheaply verifiable, so the team stays in the loop and the price reflects delivered work. Positioning leads on outcomes now; committing the invoice to outcomes is a separate founder decision.

## The competitive wedge: Real vs Synthetic

Binumi's sharpest differentiation, and a rich content vein:

> No avatars. Real video. Real trust. We use your real footage, your real experts,
> your real brand, amplified by AI, not replaced by it.

- **Not** a generative-avatar tool (Synthesia, HeyGen, Synthesys).
- **Not** a consumer or DIY editor (Canva, Veed, Descript, Lumen5, Pictory).
- The argument lands hardest in **regulated industries** (financial services, insurance, life sciences), where synthetic avatars create disclosure, IP, and trust risk.

## Site state

### This repo (the new site)
A static, hand-authored marketing site, no build step, deployed on Vercel. This is
the content hub the strategy is built around. Pages:

- `index.html`, home
- `why-it-works.html` plus six industry pages under `why-it-works/{fintech,insurance,events,tech,consultancy,industrials}/`
- `how-it-works.html`
- `insights.html`, the blog and insights listing (the acquisition layer)
- `insights/the-static-content-crisis/`, the first published insights article and the template for all future posts

The **insights hub is the engine's home.** Every new blog post is a new
`insights/{slug}/index.html` cloned from the existing article, plus a card added to
`insights.html`. See `CLAUDE.md` for the authoring steps.

### The legacy live site (binumi.com, for context)
The old live site has no blog or content hub, is Hero-contaminated in places, and
does not rank for its own thesis language or vertical terms. That is the gap this
repo and the content engine fill. Effectively zero acquisition layer exists today,
which is the opportunity.
