## Archetype

- **Primary: MAGICIAN.** Transformation, unlocking, "what becomes possible." Binumi turns static documents into video, trapped expertise into visible authority, a 100 person company into a 1,000 person voice.
- **Supporting: RULER.** Governance, brand-locking, approval engines, compliance, ISO 27001. The credibility layer that makes the magic safe to buy.
- **Eliminate: HERO.** The old site's contamination. No "growth engine," "outpace," "crush," "dominate."

**The voice rule (most important single line):**
> Always answer "what becomes possible?" before you answer "how does it work?"

The AI, platform, and 15M clips are the *engine*, never the *magic*. The magic is
what becomes possible because of them. Sell the unlock, not the tool.

## The thesis (the central intellectual asset)

> **Static Content Crisis to Systemized Expertise**

**The 90 second version:** Every B2B services and enterprise organization has the
same problem. Its best expertise (the thing that wins deals, retains employees,
aligns thousands of people) is buried in static formats nobody reads: 50 page
proposals, unopened PDFs, skimmed decks, ignored policy docs. That is the Static
Content Crisis. Meanwhile the AI industry races to replace human expertise with
synthetic avatars, which scales but does not earn trust. Binumi solves both: it
takes real expertise (real people, real footage, real documents) and systemizes it
into on-brand video that scales across every division. AI is the engine. Expertise
is the input. Trust is the output. That is Systemized Expertise.

**Three diagnostic questions** (good content hooks):
1. How many of your high-value proposals were actually read in full by clients last month?
2. If you needed to send a personalized video update to a key stakeholder in two hours, could your team do it on-brand right now?
3. What is the cost of delay when your bid team waits ten days for an agency edit while a competitor is already in the client's inbox?

## Positioning

- **Category (engine-room frame, never homepage copy):** Services-as-software for enterprise communication. A platform plus an expert team, sold as the work, not the tool. Customer-facing language stays at the unlock and outcome altitude.
- **Market stance:** Innovator and Authority. Not a challenger to Synthesia or Lumen5, a different category. The expert team is the judgment layer that makes the outcome verifiable and trustworthy where pure automation cannot.
- **Brand promise:** Scale your communications across every division, without adding headcount.
- **Primary tagline:** *Precision built for each client.*
- **Homepage hero (locked):** *Multiply Expertise. Grow Revenue.* Sub: revenue grows when your best expertise reaches more people. Binumi turns complex expertise into precision video that wins more business, brings workforces up to speed faster, and extends the reach of your most valuable experts, without increasing headcount.
- **The hero close (emergent-value line):** The speed of software, the support of a team, and outcomes neither could achieve alone. *This is the hero-level expression of the couplet. It claims 1+1=3 rather than dividing labor. Keep it.*
- **One-liner (engine room):** We don't sell you video software. We deliver the communication itself. You buy the outcome, not the tool.
- **The spine couplet:** The platform is the scale. The service is the expertise.
- **The expertise reconciliation:** Your domain knowledge is the input, our craft is what gets it out, the platform is what scales it. You are the expert in your business; we are the experts at making it land.

## The five buyer personas

Content targets these (the homepage does not, but blog and vertical content should).

1. **The Pitch Owner.** Head of Sales / Presales / CRO. Pillar: *Win the Pitch.* Fear: losing deals because pitch quality cannot stay consistent at speed.
2. **The People Architect.** Head of HR / People / L&D / Internal Comms. Pillar: *Systemize Expertise.* **The under-served buyer: highest content volume, best retention, strongest economic argument** (£40K replaces £200K+ of scattered agency spend). Give this persona at least as much content airtime as sales.
3. **The Operator.** Head of Delivery / Product / COO. Pillar: *Systemize Expertise / Execute at Pace.* Cares about brand governance, compliance, consistency at scale.
4. **The Economic Buyer.** CEO / CFO / Managing Partner. Cares about headcount math and board-level ROI.
5. **The Portfolio Operator (PE).** Operating partner or value-creation lead at a private equity firm. Buys at portfolio altitude: operational alpha, EBITDA, value leakage across PortCos. Distinct motion: a 30-day portfolio pilot across three companies to prove the model. Its own vertical, not folded into Financial Services. EBITDA-level language is correct in this room and nowhere else.

**Two axes to remember:** personas are *who you talk to*; verticals are *what
context they are in*. A "Head of HR at an insurance company" needs both. Content
flexes on both axes.

## Voice and tone

- **Energy:** calm authority. Architect, not salesperson. Not adrenaline, not hustle.
- **Default tone intensity:** about 6 out of 10. Founder LinkedIn can push to 7 (contrarian, thesis-driven). Crisis or customer issues drop to 4 (accountable, brief).
- **Sentences:** short, declarative, specific numbers, no throat-clearing.
- **Formality:** slightly formal, written like a senior partner emailing a CEO, not a SaaS startup blog.
- **American spelling** throughout (organization, systemize, optimize, program).
- **Lead on the unlock, never the diagnosis.** The Magician shows what becomes possible. It does not tell the buyer they are broken. Open on transformation, not on a problem statement.
- **Revenue is the front-door altitude, named as a specific outcome.** "Grow Revenue" is allowed and wanted: it is a plain business result a board defends in a downturn, which reframes the spend from discretionary to revenue-linked, a need not a want. What stays banned is the abstract Hero register: "growth engine," "drive growth," "outpace." Name the outcome (grow revenue); never perform the swagger.
- **Video is the solution, not the headline.** Lead on the business outcome (multiply expertise, grow revenue). Video surfaces lower on the page as the mechanism. The header earns attention on what becomes possible; video explains how.
- **Protect the People Architect when the hero leads on revenue.** Pinning the hero to revenue risks narrowing to the Pitch Owner and Economic Buyer. Keep a non-sales revenue lever in the hero ("bring workforces up to speed faster," retention, speed-to-competence) so the under-served People Architect stays in the front door.
- **We solve it; video is the mechanism.** Binumi is the agent of change. "We change that," "we get it out," not "video solves your problem."

## Vocabulary

### USE (carries archetype weight)
**Magician:** transform, unlock, boundless, instantly, engine, scale, systemize, what becomes possible, trapped expertise, real video, Systemized Expertise.
**Ruler:** precision, governance, brand-locked, approval, compliant, infrastructure, enterprise, partnership, centralized, trust, secure.
**Precision and agency (locked positioning):** adaptive, reshapes around you, tuned to your business, built for the brief, precise (not mass), the decisive moment, the moment it counts, we get it out, we change that, what your business knows, moves it forward.
**Headline outcomes (locked positioning):** multiply (the Magician headline verb: one expert becomes many, pairs with "without increasing headcount" because multiplication is not addition), grow revenue (as a specific board-level outcome, see the note below).

**Headline-verb preference:** in hero and headline position, prefer a transformation verb (multiply, unlock) over the operational "scale." "Scale" stays an owned Magician word in body copy, but as a headline verb it reads generic SaaS. "Multiply Expertise" is the feat; "scale expertise" is just an operation.

**"Grow Revenue" vs the Hero register:** "grow revenue" is sanctioned as a plain, specific outcome at board altitude. It is NOT the same as the banned abstractions. Still killed: "growth engine," "drive growth," "outpace," "supercharge growth." Name the concrete outcome; never perform the swagger.

### KILL (never use)
**Hero contamination:** growth engine, outpace, win (as in "crush the competition"), dominate, crush, hack, supercharge, race ahead, stay ahead, the future of.
**Generic SaaS:** "AI-powered video creation platform," "video creation made easy," "everything you need for video," "video isn't optional," "be video first" (acceptable only as the legacy footer line, not as a lead message), "limitless possibilities," "endless creativity."

**KILL / WATCH (locked positioning):**
- "you have a [revenue / comms / growth] problem" as an opener. Too accusatory for the Magician. The problem is subtext, never the sentence.
- "growth engine" / "drive growth" / "outpace" as Hero swagger. (Note: "grow revenue" as a named outcome is allowed; the abstract growth-swagger is not.)
- "conversion" / "sales opportunities" as front-door words. Funnel and function terms that narrow the org to sales and read as a sales tool. Route everything to revenue ("win more business") or to the moment it counts. Reserve "conversion" and "win the pitch" for the sales cut only.
- "precision tool", "AI video solution", "cloud solution". Precision is the adjective; the noun is never "tool" or "solution."
- "solve it with video." Video is the mechanism, not the magic.
- "Be video first" as a lead. Footer line only.
- "services-as-software", "SaaS", "hybrid", "software" (except descriptively, as in "the speed of software"). Engine-room and investor language only. Never as a claim that Binumi is software.

## The Real vs Synthetic wedge (a core content vein)

> No avatars. Real video. Real trust. Amplified by AI, not replaced by it.

Three supporting arguments:
- **Compliance.** In regulated industries, synthetic avatars create disclosure, IP, and reputational risk. Real video does not.
- **Trust.** Buyers and employees can tell when something is synthetic; the uncanny valley undermines the message.
- **IP.** A generative avatar commoditises your expertise into something anyone could make; real video protects and amplifies what is actually yours.

## The False Choice (the third named position)

Beside the Static Content Crisis (the problem) and Real vs Synthetic (the trust
wedge) sits the market-fatigue wedge:

> Everyone hands you a tool or an agency, and both leave you holding the problem.
> The platform sits unused because a tool was never going to do the thinking; the
> agency is slow and stops at the retainer. Binumi refuses the choice.

This is the customer-facing face of the services-as-software bet. It lives in the
homepage expertise section, ahead of the precision triad, and is strong
founder-LinkedIn fuel at a 7.

## Headline and CTA conventions

- **Headlines** do two of three things at once: name the buyer's pain, state the transformation, carry the math.
- **CTAs** are invitations into transformation, never requests for time. Use "Start your proof of value" or "See what your [team] could do." Never "Book a demo" as a primary CTA.
- **Blog and LinkedIn:** close on a question, not a hard CTA. Engagement is the goal; conversations come from comments, not links. (The on-page article CTA block at the foot of a post is the one exception, see `CLAUDE.md`.)
- **The homepage de-centering:** the signature line "The best video is already inside your business" is now de-centered on the homepage to "Your best expertise is already inside your business," so the page leads on expertise and revenue, not video. Video stays the mechanism, surfaced lower. The original line remains the pullquote in the published Static Content Crisis article; decide later whether to align that article to the expertise-led phrasing or leave it as the video-led origin of the idea.

## The content system (how the brand ships)

The site is the hub. Everything is produced once and works across every surface.

- **One idea, many channels.** Each flagship idea is built once: a blog post (the hub), a video that lives on the post, the same blog reposted to LinkedIn by the brand, founder and senior-team cuts, and a nurture email.
- **Video on everything.** Every blog, every social post, and every email ships with video. Nothing goes out video-less.
- **Video length caps.** Blog and YouTube master video up to 90 seconds. Every LinkedIn video is a cut and never exceeds 45 seconds (hard cap). Email reuses the 45 second cut.
- **The brand reposts every blog to LinkedIn** the day it lands.
- **No paid media.** Organic (blog plus SEO and GEO, video, LinkedIn, email) is the number one growth channel. There is no paid layer.
- **The recipe for any asset:** take the thesis, run it through one pillar and one persona, open on what becomes possible, land one named proof point (never a borrowed statistic), close on a question. Kill list barred. American spelling holds.

### Editorial categories
- **Thesis pieces.** Flagship arguments developing Static Content Crisis to Systemized Expertise. Quarterly cornerstones.
- **Vertical pieces.** The same thinking told for each of the six verticals. The heavy lift and the highest payoff for industry search and GEO.
- **Field notes.** Named-client deep dives (Fujitsu, Knight Frank, Generali, S2). The credibility layer.
- **Real vs Synthetic.** POV pieces on why generative-avatar video fails in regulated industries.
- **HR and Internal Comms.** Targeted at the People Architect, the under-served buyer.

## What "elite" means (the bar all content is held to)

- **Restraint.** A confident, quiet brand that does not try to say everything at once.
- **Authority.** A genuinely deep body of published thinking that proves Binumi belongs in the room.
- **Guidance.** Taking a serious buyer straight to what matters, without making them dig.

Elite is not more features or louder claims. It is depth, restraint, and a confident
point of view. The content engine is how Binumi earns the authority half.
