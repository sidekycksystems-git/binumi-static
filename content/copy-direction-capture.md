## 1. The hero, as shipped (the new reference)

**H1:** Scale Expertise. Grow Revenue.

**Lead:** Your next revenue line is already on the payroll. Revenue is capped by
how many rooms your best experts can be in. The conventional fixes, more
salespeople or more agencies, add cost faster than they add revenue. Binumi
turns the expertise you already employ into precision video that wins more
business, brings revenue-critical capability up to speed faster, and reaches
every market at once, without increasing headcount.

**Primary CTA:** Start your proof of value

### What changed from the locked handoff, and why it matters for the audit
- **Verb: "Scale", not "Multiply".** The earlier doctrine preferred a
  transformation verb over "scale" in hero position. That preference is now
  OVERRIDDEN on the homepage. Site-wide implication: "scale" is sanctioned in
  headline position. It does NOT need to be purged elsewhere, but it should not
  be the only verb the site leans on. (Note the tension: "scale" also appears
  in the couplet "the platform is the scale". Acceptable, but watch for the
  word going thin through overuse on vertical pages.)
- **The payroll line is the lead hook.** "Your next revenue line is already on
  the payroll" is now a core positioning device: hidden value, revealed
  (Magician), expressed in pure revenue terms. Vertical and how-it-works copy
  should echo its LOGIC (the value is already inside the business, we release
  it) without copying the sentence.
- **Revenue is the sole front-door altitude.** Every benefit clause resolves to
  revenue: "wins more business", "revenue-critical capability up to speed
  faster", "reaches every market". The People Architect is kept in via
  "revenue-critical capability up to speed faster" (capability and ramp speed,
  expressed as revenue) rather than a separate workforce outcome.

## 2. The tone, named (what the auditor is matching against)

Six observable rules, all visible in the shipped copy:

1. **Short declaratives, full stops over commas-for-drama.** "Video isn't a
   project. It's a capability." "The work is complex. The story shouldn't be."
   Two-beat structures, the second beat turning the first.
2. **The value is already inside the business; Binumi releases it.** "The best
   video is already inside your business." "Your next revenue line is already on
   the payroll." "Take your expertise out of the deck." This is the spine
   sentence-shape. Every page should have at least one line in it.
3. **Video is the mechanism, never the headline.** Headlines lead on the
   business outcome or the capability; video surfaces lower as the how. "Video
   isn't a project. It's a capability" is allowed because it RE-FRAMES video as
   a capability, it does not sell video as the thing.
4. **Plain nouns, no swagger.** "wins the pitch and lands the engagement",
   "make complex technology obvious". No "growth engine", "supercharge",
   "transform your business", "unlock". Magician through revelation, not through
   magic words.
5. **Proof is named-client and specific.** "100+ slide decks into video. £1M+
   revenue influenced." Never a borrowed industry statistic, never a vague
   superlative.
6. **Sentence-case headers.** Every H2 on the site is sentence case, not title
   case. ("Built for six industries." not "Built For Six Industries.")

### The "platform and service" couplet
"The platform is the scale. The service is the expertise." remains the spine.
On the homepage it lives in the footer tagline and the how-it-works framing
("One system, run by a platform and a team"). Every vertical page should land
this idea once, in its own words or verbatim.

## 3. The audit checklist (apply to every non-homepage page)

For each page, the auditor checks and reports:

**A. Altitude and outcome**
- [ ] Does the hero lead on a business outcome or capability, NOT on video?
- [ ] Does the page resolve to revenue or revenue-critical capability, not to
      "better content" or "engagement" as an end in itself?
- [ ] Is it pitched at C-suite/enterprise altitude, not at a department or a
      sales persona?

**B. The spine**
- [ ] Is there at least one "the value is already inside your business" line?
- [ ] Does the platform-and-service couplet land once on the page?

**C. Tone**
- [ ] Short declaratives, two-beat turns, no em-dashes?
- [ ] Sentence-case headers throughout?
- [ ] Plain nouns, zero Hero vocabulary? (Flag every instance of: growth engine,
      supercharge, unlock, transform your business, drive growth, outpace,
      dominate, game-changing, revolutionize, be video first outside the footer.)

**D. Proof**
- [ ] Every figure is a named-client outcome, no borrowed statistics?
- [ ] Every figure matches the approved set? (FIGURE GATE, see section 5.)

**E. CTA and close**
- [ ] Primary CTA reads "Start your proof of value", never "Start your pilot",
      never "Book a demo"?
- [ ] Does the page close on a question or a proof-of-value invitation, not a
      hard transactional CTA? (Blog and LinkedIn close on a question; landing
      pages close on proof-of-value.)

**F. Spelling and mechanics**
- [ ] American spelling throughout?
- [ ] No em-dashes anywhere?

## 4. Known site-wide copy issues to resolve in the audit

Surfaced from the shipped homepage and the live page read:

- **"Be video first" in the footer.** This is the sanctioned footer-only home
  for the legacy line. ALLOWED. The audit confirms it appears ONLY in the
  footer and nowhere else on any page.
- **The two vertical descriptions differ between the homepage `#verticals`
  grid and the `Built for six industries` grid.** The homepage carries two
  different one-liners per industry (e.g. FinTech: "Turn complex products and
  compliance-heavy messages into video clear enough for any audience" vs "Move
  at the speed of fintech. Without losing trust."). The audit should confirm
  the vertical PAGES use copy consistent with the punchier `Built for six
  industries` set, and flag any drift to the older, more video-led phrasing.
- **FinTech and Events proof gaps.** Still the highest-priority content
  investment. FinTech vertical copy currently leans on cross-vertical names,
  not a named FinTech client. Flag, do not paper over.
- **"S2 Partnership: About 80% fewer onboarding support calls."** This figure
  is now live on the homepage ledger. It is a specific named-client outcome,
  good. It passes through the figure gate like every other.
