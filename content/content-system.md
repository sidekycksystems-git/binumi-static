# Binumi Content System

> The repeatable editorial engine. How one idea becomes a week of assets across
> every surface, with no paid media behind it. Read with `brand.md` and
> `product.md`. House rules: British spelling, no em-dashes.

## The model: one idea, many channels

Every flagship idea is produced once and works across every surface at once.

1. **Blog post**, the hub. The crawlable, citable SEO and GEO surface. Depth lives here. (`insights/{slug}/`.)
2. **Video**, on the post. The YouTube and GEO surface, and proof of Binumi's own thesis (a company systemizing its expertise into video). Master up to 90 seconds.
3. **Brand repost to LinkedIn**, the day the blog lands. The company page posts every blog.
4. **Founder and team cuts**, LinkedIn. The relationship and authority layer, in named voices. Each cut 45 seconds maximum.
5. **Nurture email**, the slow capture layer for the long enterprise cycle.

A 32 person team does not run five content streams. It runs one idea, five ways.

### Non-negotiables
- **Video on everything.** Every blog, post, and email ships with video.
- **Caps.** Blog and YouTube master up to 90s. Every LinkedIn video 45s, hard cap. Email reuses the 45s cut.
- **No paid media.** Organic is the number one growth channel.
- **The recipe.** Thesis, run through one pillar and one persona, open on what becomes possible, one named proof point, close on a question. Kill list barred.

## Editorial categories
- **Thesis pieces.** Cornerstone arguments developing Static Content Crisis to Systemized Expertise. Quarterly.
- **Vertical pieces.** The thesis told for each of the six verticals. Heavy lift, highest payoff for industry search and GEO.
- **Field notes.** Named-client deep dives (Fujitsu, Knight Frank, Generali, S2). The credibility layer.
- **Real vs Synthetic.** POV on why generative-avatar video fails in regulated industries. Needs no client logo, safe on founder channels.
- **HR and Internal Comms.** For the People Architect, the under-served buyer. Most airtime.

## Vertical sequencing (recommended)
Lead by proof strength, and lead with what doubles as LinkedIn fuel.
1. **Knight Frank proof** (ex-Real Estate): still the strongest packaged proof, the only attributed testimonial, the 5x LinkedIn lift. Real Estate is retired as a vertical, so this now anchors the homepage and About page rather than a dedicated industry page, and doubles as LinkedIn fuel.
2. **Tech** (Fujitsu): the cleanest Win the Pitch story (£1M+, 56 page deck to video).
3. **Insurance** (Generali) and **Consultancy** (S2): strong; S2 anchors the HR and onboarding content.
4. **Industrials**: proof exists but less packaged.
5. **FinTech and Events, the two proof gaps, handled deliberately.** FinTech: lead with the Real vs Synthetic / regulated angle (compliance, IP, trust), which needs no client logo. Events: replaced Real Estate in the lineup and uses the bracketed interim treatment until a named events client lands. Commissioning proof for both is a parallel workstream.

## LinkedIn rhythm
Weekly, across the brand page and two named posters, founder-weighted.
- **Monday:** thesis (a poster, to camera).
- **Wednesday:** named-client proof (brand, clip plus figure).
- **Friday:** contrarian POV (Real vs Synthetic).
The brand also reposts the week's blog the day it lands. Every post closes on a
question; engagement and comments are the goal, not click-through.

## Email rhythm
A weekly nurture sequence for anyone who downloads the gated asset (the obvious
first lead magnet: an interactive version of the three diagnostic questions, "How
much of your expertise is trapped?"). Each email carries video and closes on a
question. Only the final email names a next step (the proof of value).

## Cadence (starting point, then ramp)
- **Blogs:** start at 2 flagships per month, ramp to weekly as the engine proves out.
- **LinkedIn:** 3 posts per week continuous, plus the brand blog repost.
- **Email:** one nurture send per week.
- **Quarterly flagship:** a gated report ("The State of B2B Video Communications"), not in the first quarter.

## Measurement (first two quarters)
Judge on leading indicators, not lead volume (enterprise cycles are long): founder
LinkedIn engagement and inbound conversations, GEO citations, organic vertical
rankings, and proof-of-value enquiries.
