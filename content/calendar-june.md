# Binumi Content Calendar, June (worked example)

> One idea a week, built from a single master video, working across every surface.
> Brand reposts every blog. Video on everything. Blog video up to 90s, LinkedIn
> video 45s hard cap. No paid media. House rules: British spelling, no em-dashes.
> All client figures below are placeholders to confirm against `product.md` and
> the latest approved numbers before anything publishes.

## Mock LinkedIn posts (the corrected voice)
Magician open, named proof, no Hero words, British spelling, closes on a question.

**Poster 1, Wk1 thesis**
> Your best thinking is sitting in a 50 page proposal that the client skimmed for ninety seconds and never opened again.
> That is the Static Content Crisis. The expertise that wins the deal, retains the team, aligns a thousand people, is buried in formats built to be filed, not felt.
> What becomes possible when that same expertise is on-brand video any division can send in two hours? You stop sounding like your headcount and start sounding like your ambition.
> How much of what your company actually knows is trapped in documents nobody finished reading?

**Brand, Wk2 proof**
> A 56 page deck does not win the room. The story inside it does.
> A Tech client took the proposal that was putting buyers to sleep and turned it into one video that opened the pitch. The deal moved, and post-meeting engagement lifted 5x. (confirm figure)
> Same expertise. Different format. The platform is the scale, the service is the expertise.
> When your bid team needs the pitch on-brand by tomorrow, can they do it today?

**Poster 2, Wk3 thesis**
> We spend months recruiting the right person, then four hours onboarding them. Then we wonder why week one decides whether they stay.
> The best companies have stopped treating onboarding as a checklist and started treating it as a product: structured, on-brand, personal, built to scale across every new starter.
> A Consultancy that made the shift cut onboarding support calls by around 80%. (confirm) The first seven days became something people remember, not something they survive.
> What does your first day actually communicate about the company someone just joined?

**Poster 1, Wk4 contrarian**
> The industry is racing to replace experts with synthetic avatars. It scales. It just does not earn trust.
> In regulated, high stakes work, the face on screen is a signal. A real person, real footage, real expertise, made to scale, says something a generated avatar never will: this is who we actually are.
> Scale and trust were never meant to be a trade off.
> Would you stake your brand's credibility on a face that does not exist?

## Two master video beat sheets
Master runs up to 90s and lives on the blog. The LinkedIn cut is beats 1 and 4,
45s maximum.

### Week 1 master, The Static Content Crisis (assembled, 16:9)
- **0 to 15s, what becomes possible.** On screen: a thick printed proposal closing shut. VO: "Imagine every department in your company communicating like it had a thousand people. Today, most of that expertise is locked in a document nobody finished."
- **15 to 50s, the transformation.** On screen: the static deck pages lift off the page and reassemble into a branded video frame. VO: "This is the Static Content Crisis. Real expertise, trapped in static formats. We take the real thing, your people, your footage, your documents, and systemize it into on-brand video that scales across every division, in two hours, not two weeks."
- **50 to 72s, one named proof.** On screen: a single figure animates up. VO: "One client turned a proposal nobody read into a video that moved the deal, and lifted engagement 5x." (confirm figure)
- **72 to 90s, the question.** On screen: logo, one line, no button. VO: "How much of what your company knows is trapped in something nobody reads?"
- **LinkedIn cut:** beat 2 plus beat 4, 45s, captioned. Brand cut posts the blog; poster cut is the to camera open.

### Week 4 master, Real vs Synthetic (to camera, 16:9)
- **0 to 15s, what becomes possible.** Founder to camera, calm, single light. "You can scale your communications across every division without losing the one thing that makes people trust you. The fact that you are real."
- **15 to 50s, the transformation.** Hold on speaker. "The industry is racing to replace experts with synthetic avatars. It scales, but it does not earn trust. In regulated work, the face on screen is a signal. We scale the real thing instead: real people, real expertise, made repeatable across every market you operate in."
- **50 to 72s, one named proof.** Brief cutaway to a real branded clip. "That is why an Insurance client could roll out the same trusted message across multiple markets, fast, and have it still feel like them." (confirm detail)
- **72 to 90s, the question.** Speaker holds, logo fades up. "So before you automate the face of your brand, ask yourself: would you trust one that does not exist?"
- **LinkedIn cut:** beat 1 plus beat 4, 45s. Easiest cut of the month.
